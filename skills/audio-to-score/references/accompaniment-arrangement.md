# Accompaniment Arrangement

## Boundary

Treat accompaniment as generated output derived from approved transcription. Set `provenance` to `generated` on the arrangement and every generated layer.

Do not copy uncertain source notes into the arrangement as if they were verified. Use approved melody, bass, harmony, meter, tempo, groove, and form as inputs.

## Arrangement Plan

Define before generating notes:

- target instrument or ensemble,
- difficulty and playable range,
- texture per section and occurrence,
- harmonic rhythm,
- bass role,
- rhythmic vocabulary,
- density curve,
- intro, fill, turnaround, transition, and ending policy,
- melody-collision policy.

Use neutral section IDs internally even when semantic labels are available.

## Piano

- Keep hand ranges and span practical for the selected difficulty.
- Prefer smooth voice leading over root-position repetition.
- Leave register and rhythmic space for the melody.
- Make bass motion explicit when it defines inversion or harmonic direction.
- Vary density by form role without inventing unsupported form changes.

## Guitar

- Respect tuning, capo, fret reach, open-string policy, and requested skill level.
- Preserve performance-significant slash basses and harmonic rhythm.
- Choose a consistent strumming, arpeggio, or hybrid texture per phrase unless a form transition supports a change.

## Groove

Use observed groove as a reference, not a source-note claim.

- Record groove evidence references.
- Quantize a playable pattern while preserving characteristic syncopation.
- Avoid copying transient noise or drum bleed into harmonic rhythm.

## Review

Check playability, melody clearance, voice leading, section contrast, cadence support, bass coherence, and ending behavior. Require separate arrangement approval. A valid transcription does not imply an approved arrangement.
